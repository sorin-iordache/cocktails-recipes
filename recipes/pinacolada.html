Pina Colada

Description

The Pina Colada is a tropical classic that has been satisfying vacationers and Tiki aficionados since the 1950s. Despite its reputation as a blender-heavy drink, the Pina Colada has a rich history and a place in the sun.

Legend has it that Ramon “Monchito” Marrero, the head barman at the Caribe Hilton in Old San Juan, Puerto Rico, first mixed this delightful concoction in 1954. Craft-focused bartenders have reclaimed the original recipe, emphasizing solid ingredients and proper proportions. The result? A sweet yet balanced cocktail that transports you to a sunny beach with every sip

Ingredients

  • Rum

    • he base spirit of the Pina Colada, light rum provides the necessary alcoholic kick.

  • Cream of Coconut

    • This creamy, sweet ingredient adds richness and coconut flavor to the cocktail.

  • Pineapple Juice

    • Fresh pineapple juice brings tropical sweetness and tanginess.

  • Freshly Squeezed Lime Juice

    • A splash of lime juice balances the sweetness and adds a zesty twist.

  • Garnish

    • A pineapple wedge and a pineapple leaf complete the presentation.

Directions

  1. Combine Ingredients. Add 2 ounces of light rum, 1 1/2 ounces of cream of coconut, 1 1/2 ounces of pineapple juice, and 1/2 ounce of freshly squeezed lime juice to a shaker filled with ice.

  2. Shake Vigorously. Shake the mixture vigorously for 20 to 30 seconds.

  3. Strain and Serve. Strain the cocktail into a chilled Hurricane glass filled with pebble ice.

  4. Garnish. Place a pineapple wedge and a pineapple leaf on top.

  5. Enjoy. Sip your Piña Colada and let the tropical vibes transport you to paradise!
